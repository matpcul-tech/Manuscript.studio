- Generic AI prose that sounds robotic

## Scene Descriptions for Video Production

### Scene A -- The Problem (frustration)
Writer at laptop, face falling as they read AI output. Close-up of text that is generic, robotic, over-explained. Red highlights. Ctrl-Z. The writer leans back. Sighs.
*Mood: dim room, amber desk lamp, evening*

### Scene B -- Voice Training
App on screen. Writer pastes their own writing into a clean text area. Progress indicator appears. A voice profile card materializes -- showing sentence length graph, vocabulary density, rhythm markers.
*Mood: bright, clean, morning light*

### Scene C -- Quick Draft generating
The writer types one paragraph describing their book. Clicks Generate. Chapter list appears. Chapters fill in one by one. Progress bar. The writer leans in -- reading. They smile.
*Mood: daylight, focused, satisfying*

### Scene D -- Scene editor
Split view: outline on left, editor on right. Writer reads a paragraph. It sounds like them. They edit two words, save, continue. No big rewrites needed.
*Mood: productive, natural flow, calm*

### Scene E -- Launch stage
Publishing Pack tabs across the top. KDP Description generates. Three variants appear side by side. Writer picks one. Copies it. Switches to KDP in another tab. Pastes. Checkbox turns green. Next field.
*Mood: efficient, professional, satisfying*

### Scene F -- Logo close-out
Clean white background. M mark in blue gradient, square with rounded corners. "Manuscript Studio" wordmark. URL below. Tagline fades in: Write your book. In your voice.

## Brand Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Say "your voice" frequently -- that is the core promise
- Show the actual app interface (it looks good)
- Mention Amazon KDP by name -- that is where users publish
- Emphasize speed from idea to published book
- Show real-looking prose output (not lorem ipsum)

**Don't:**
- Use the word "revolutionary" or "game-changing"
- Say "AI-powered" as a selling point -- the voice angle is the sell
- Show only desktop -- app works on mobile too
- Promise the app writes the book for you -- it writes WITH you
- Use em dashes in any on-screen text
